## 1. Purpose

The parties wish to explore a potential business relationship related
to the Titan X5-B GPU intellectual property (the "Purpose"). In
connection with the Purpose, each party may disclose confidential
information to the other.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed
by one party ("Disclosing Party") to the other ("Receiving Party"),
whether disclosed orally, in writing, or by other means, that is
either:

1. Marked or identified as "confidential" or "proprietary" at the
   time of disclosure; or

2. By its nature, would reasonably be considered confidential,
   including but not limited to:
   - Source code (SystemVerilog RTL, testbenches, scripts)
   - Architecture and microarchitecture specifications
   - Verification plans, coverage reports, bug databases
   - Synthesis reports, gate counts, timing reports
   - FPGA utilization reports
   - Roadmaps, pricing, business plans
   - Customer lists, partner lists
   - Trade secrets, know-how, techniques

## 3. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Receiving Party agrees to:

1. Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose stated above.

2. Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of
   care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no
   less than reasonable care.

3. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without
   the Disclosing Party's prior written consent.

4. Limit access to Confidential Information to its employees, agents,
   and contractors who:
   - Have a need to know for the Purpose
   - Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective
     as those in this Agreement

5. Not reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any disclosed
   materials (except for RTL source code explicitly provided for
   evaluation, which may be analyzed for evaluation purposes).

6. Not use Confidential Information to develop competing products
   during the term of this Agreement and for 2 years thereafter.

## 4. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving
   Party;

2. Was known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, as evidenced
   by written records;

3. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of
   or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

4. Is rightfully received from a third party without a breach of any
   confidentiality obligation; or

5. Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or government
   regulation, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party
   prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order.

## 5. Term

This Agreement is effective for 1 year from the Effective Date, and
the confidentiality obligations survive for 5 years after the
Effective Date, regardless of whether the parties enter into a
business relationship.

## 6. Return or Destruction of Materials

Upon written request from the Disclosing Party, or upon termination
of discussions, the Receiving Party will, within 30 days:

1. Return all originals and copies of Confidential Information; or
2. Certify in writing that all Confidential Information has been
   destroyed.

The Receiving Party may retain one (1) copy solely for archival
purposes, subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

## 7. No License

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any license,
right, or interest in the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information,
intellectual property, or trade secrets, except the right to evaluate
for the Purpose. All Confidential Information remains the sole
property of the Disclosing Party.

## 8. No Obligation to Proceed

This Agreement does not obligate either party to enter into any
further agreement or business relationship. Either party may
terminate discussions at any time, for any reason, with written notice.

## 9. Equitable Relief

The Receiving Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may
cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages are inadequate.
The Disclosing Party may seek injunctive relief or specific
performance, in addition to any other remedies available at law or
equity.

## 10. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of India, without regard to
conflict of laws principles. Any dispute shall be resolved in the
courts of [Your City], India, or in the courts of [Counterparty City]
if Party B is the Disclosing Party seeking enforcement.

## 11. Miscellaneous

1. This Agreement is the entire agreement between the parties
   regarding its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements.

2. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

3. If any provision is found unenforceable, the remainder stays in
   full force and effect.

4. Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's
   written consent.

5. This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including
   electronically.
